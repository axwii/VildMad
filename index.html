Foraging in cities and towns

With its asphalt and concrete makeup, a town resembles a rocky landscape, though parks,
gardens and abandoned fields add their patches of green.
Many of those green spaces are manicured and carefully tended, which means you won't find many edible plants there.
Instead, seek out spots where nature has been left to its own devices, especially those further from urban centers.
Use caution when foraging along major roads or near factories where there is a risk of pollution,
and avoid places where herbicides are sprayed.

Get foraging with VILD MAD

Foraging doesn’t require extensive training or expertise to yield safe, delicious bounty.
All you need is a little planning, a thoughtful attitude, and the full use of your senses.
To make your inaugural trip as fruitful as possible, do the following before you start foraging:

• Select a few ingredients you want to find.
• Read and study the pictures for each ingredient carefully so that you can recognize your desired plants in the wild.
